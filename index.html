WePick

Helping Answer The Greatest Questions of our Generation

  • About the Project

    What started as a UX Bootcamp project turned into my side passion project. This app allowed me to learn about almost the entire UX design process. From working as a team and presenting it as a low-fi prototype, to flying solo on the high-fidelity design, this project has helped me immensely.

  • The Team

    I teamed up with several other classmates on this UX boot camp project. We worked together throughout the research phase, and I did the majority of the low-fidelity prototyping and all of the high-fidelity design.

  • The Tools

    In this project, the prototyping and design work was done in Adobe XD, documents were stored in Google Drive, brainstorming and wireframing took place in Miro, and Zoom was how we communicated.

  • The Challenge

    As long as streaming movies and TV shows existed, the question “What do you want to watch?” has been the start of many an argument. The same feeling surrounds the question, “Where do you want to eat?”. Everyone has experience with these questions. It can be a paralyzing task! Instead of wasting precious time together trying to make a decision, is there a way that we could help people decide where to eat or what to watch faster and easier? This was our quest. A noble one at that. A way to difinitively answer “Where do you want to eat?” or “What do you want to watch?”.

  • Empathize

    First, we had to think about who would be using this app. With our two main activities, going out to eat and streaming a movie or TV show online, we started with what demographics most often do that activity.

Key Metrics

53%

The percentage of millenials that go out to eat at least once a week. This is 10% higher than any other generation.

75%

The percentage of 18-44 year-olds that have a current Netflix subscription. Compared to 44% of those aged 65 and above.

74%

The percentage of millenials that download at least one app a month.

  • These facts made us want to focus more on the millenial age when we were thinking about the design of this app.
